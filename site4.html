Golden State Warriors

The Golden State Warriors are an NBA professional basketball team based in San Francisco, California. They are one of the league's original franchises and are tied for the third-most championships.

Titles: 7 NBA Championships.

Location: San Francisco, California (originally Philadelphia Warriors, moved to San Francisco Bay Area in the 1960s).

Historical Records: Hold the best all-time regular season record (73-9 in 2015-16) and the best playoff record (16-1 in 2017).

Key Eras and Stars:

Philadelphia Warriors (1946–1962): Won the inaugural BAA championship (1947) and another title in 1956. Starred Wilt Chamberlain, who famously scored 100 points in 1962.

Oakland/San Francisco (1970s): Won their first West Coast title in 1975, led by Rick Barry.

Run T-M-C (Late 1980s–Early 1990s): Exciting, fast-paced team featuring Tim Hardaway, Mitch Richmond, and Chris Mullin.

The Dynasty Era (2015–Present): Led by the core of Stephen Curry (franchise player), Klay Thompson, and Draymond Green, and coached by Steve Kerr. They won four championships in eight years (2015, 2017, 2018, 2022).
